P.A.U.S.E

The pomodoro technique was developed by Italian Francesco Cirillo to improve productivity in his university studies, based himself on the idea that workflows divided into blocks can improve brain agility and stimulate focus. After much research, Cirillo came up with the 25 minutes period as the ideal time for these blocks of temp. Since then, has helped people and work teams to remain focused on their daily activities, thus improving their productive performance.

The technique is simple:

  • Make a list of tasks that are pending.
  • Set a timer for 25 minutes.
  • Choose one of the tasks and work on it without interruption.
  • Work on the chosen task until the alarm goes off.
  • take a break for 5 minutes(relax).
  • Resume work after the break for another “pomodoro”(25 minutes).
  • Every four “pomodoros”, take a longer break: 15 minutes before returning to work.
